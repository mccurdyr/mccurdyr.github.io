Ryan McCurdy

About Me!

Ryan McCurdy is a professional developer that has been molded by the corporate world of marketing and business operations. After spending a number of years working synchronously with people and computers, and a many more playing video games at his leisure, he decided it was time to understand how everything worked "under the hood."

A total tech nerd, his curiosity drives forward his innate desire to learn, build, and play. Away from the keyboard and controller, Ryan likes to spend his time in nature, exploring the parks in Greater Cleveland and beyond.

Whether behind a keyboard, drum kit, or mixing bowl, Ryan's incessant drive to constantly improve is as asset to any team he may be a part of. Whether fighting hordes of logic problems, or sailing the high seas of Google, compassion pushes him to make connections with his teammates, to better understand one another and aid in solving problems together!

Projects

Seed & Feed

Seed & Feed is a modern garden planner and recipe finder built with the new gardener in mind. This was my final project for our coding bootcamp. The team and I tackled many challenges along the way, but we're extremely proud of how it turned out! It is scalable, and we can see an opportunity to monotize. This app was a great demonstration of what software development empowers people to do as a team.



TV Show Review Site

This was my first time coding in a group setting, and I had an absolute blast with it! We were tasked with creating a webapp through which users could review a topic. Our group took a vote, and The Magic Rockets were off to the races! We used MVC patterns through Spring Boot and Thymeleaf to collaborate on building this app. I think we did pretty well!



Guessing Game

This one was a test of my ability to use if/else statements in a creative and adaptable manner! It may not have been dangerous OR adventurous, but it solidified my understanding of conditional statements.



Virtual Pet Shop

This one was a bit of a doozy. I built a console game in which the player manages the needs of their Virtual Pets. This really established my solid understanding of classes, objects, and how they're able to interact with one another at a simple, yet versatile manner.
